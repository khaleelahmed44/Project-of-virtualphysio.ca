How an Ottawa senior improved balance and reduced falls.

This case study outlines the initial problem, treatment approach and outcome for a home-based physiotherapy plan focused on safer movement and confidence.

Initial Problem

An older adult in Ottawa was experiencing frequent balance losses, fear of falling and difficulty moving independently after a period of reduced activity. Stairs, bathroom transfers and walking short distances felt unsafe, and the patient wanted practical guidance to regain confidence at home.

Treatment Approach

  • Performed an in-home assessment of mobility, balance and strength limitations.
  • Reviewed environmental risks and suggested subtle home modifications.
  • Prescribed a progressive balance and lower-body strengthening program tailored to the patient’s home routines.
  • Delivered coaching on safe transfers, posture and activity pacing through repeated in-home visits.
  • Monitored progress with weekly check-ins and adjusted the plan based on symptoms and functional goals.

Outcome

The patient reported improved confidence walking in their home, fewer episodes of instability, and greater independence with daily tasks. Balance, strength and movement safety improved enough to reduce fall risk and promote a more active lifestyle.
